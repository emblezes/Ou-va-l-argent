## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Key Finding:** Most countries operate MIXED pension systems combining PAYG and funded components. Pure systems are rare.

**Verification Confidence:** HIGH (based on OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025, World Bank data, official government sources)

**Critical Nuances:**
- The majority of OECD countries combine PAYG first-pillar pensions with funded second/third pillars
- "Mandatory funded" systems exist in 19 of 38 OECD countries (2024)
- Even Chile's "pure capitalization" system underwent major reform in 2025, becoming mixed
- Classification depends on which pillar is DOMINANT in terms of replacement rate and coverage

## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. CHILE IS NO LONGER PURELY CAPITALIZATION ‚ùå

**Critical Update:** As of March 2025, Chile's pension system is now MIXED, combining individual capitalization (AFPs) with a new Social Security Pension Insurance (SSPI) component. The claim that "Chile has a pure capitalization system" is **OUTDATED**.

### 2. FRANCE IS PURELY PAYG (R√âPARTITION) ‚úÖ

**Confirmed:** France operates a pure pay-as-you-go system for both basic pensions AND supplementary pensions (AGIRC-ARRCO). The second pillar AGIRC-ARRCO is often misunderstood as funded, but it explicitly operates on a PAYG basis where current contributions immediately finance current retirees.

### 3. MOST COUNTRIES HAVE MIXED SYSTEMS ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Key Insight:** The binary classification "capitalization vs PAYG" is oversimplified. According to OECD Pensions at a Glance 2025:
- 19 of 38 OECD countries have mandatory or quasi-mandatory funded components
- Most combine PAYG first-pillar basic pensions with funded second/third pillars
- The trend is towards mixed systems rather than pure models

### 4. MANDATORY FUNDED PENSION COUNTRIES (Complete List)

**Confirmed Mandatory Funded Systems (2024-2025):**

**Pure or Dominant Funded:**
1. Singapore (CPF)
2. Australia (Superannuation)
3. Switzerland (BVG/LPP 2nd pillar)
4. Mexico (AFOREs)

**Quasi-Mandatory (90%+ coverage):**
5. Netherlands (occupational pensions)
6. Denmark (ATP + occupational)
7. Iceland (occupational pensions)
8. Finland (occupational pensions)

**Auto-Enrollment / Mixed:**
9. United Kingdom (NEST auto-enrollment)

**Formerly Pure, Now Mixed:**

12-19. Various OECD countries with smaller mandatory funded pillars (Israel, Estonia, Lithuania, etc.)

**Important:** NDC systems are PAYG (no actual investment) but mimic funded DC systems through notional accounts. They should be classified as PAYG, not funded.

### 6. WORLD BANK THREE-PILLAR FRAMEWORK

**Historical Context:**
- World Bank's 1994 report "Averting the Old Age Crisis" promoted three-pillar systems
- Influenced 18+ countries to adopt funded mandatory second pillars
- Based on Chile's 1981 reform success
- Baltic states, Eastern Europe adopted model in 1990s-2000s
- Mixed results: some countries (Poland) reversed course, others (Netherlands, Denmark) succeeded

## FACT-CHECK VERDICTS

### Claim 1: "Chile has a pure capitalization pension system"
**Verdict:** ‚ùå FALSE (as of 2025)
**Correction:** Chile HAD a pure capitalization system (1981-2025) but implemented major reform in March 2025 creating a mixed system with both individual capitalization (AFPs) and Social Security Pension Insurance (SSPI).

### Claim 2: "France has a pure r√©partition (PAYG) system"
**Verdict:** ‚úÖ TRUE
**Confirmation:** Both basic pensions and supplementary pensions (AGIRC-ARRCO) operate on pay-as-you-go basis. Funded components are minimal (voluntary third pillar only).

### Claim 3: "Australia has mandatory private pension savings"
**Verdict:** ‚úÖ TRUE
**Confirmation:** Superannuation Guarantee is mandatory at 12% (2025), managed by private fund managers, paid by employers.

### Claim 4: "Singapore CPF is a mandatory funded system"
**Verdict:** ‚úÖ TRUE
**Confirmation:** CPF is compulsory savings scheme with individual accounts, fully funded, no PAYG component.

### Claim 5: "Netherlands has a mandatory funded second pillar"
**Verdict:** ‚ö†Ô∏è PARTIALLY TRUE (quasi-mandatory)
**Nuance:** Not legally mandatory for all, but 90%+ coverage through collective labor agreements makes it effectively mandatory. Correctly classified as "quasi-mandatory."

### Claim 6: "Most developed countries are moving away from PAYG to funded systems"
**Verdict:** ‚ö†Ô∏è MIXED / NUANCED
**Reality:** The trend is towards MIXED systems, not pure funded systems. While funded components are growing in many countries (UK auto-enrollment, Netherlands expansion), pure PAYG systems (France, Germany, Spain) remain resistant to major structural reform. Some countries (Poland 2014) even moved away from funded systems.
